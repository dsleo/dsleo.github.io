Random walk with a weak drift toward an invisible stencil.
ⓘ

Idea. First, the page draws an invisible outline of “404” on a hidden canvas and records lots of points along that outline.

Then it releases many “walkers” with position \(x_t \in \mathbb{R}^2\) and velocity \(v_t\). At each step the velocity is damped and nudged toward a target outline point \(p\): \[ v_{t+1} = \rho v_t + \eta (p - x_t) + \varepsilon_t, \qquad x_{t+1} = x_t + v_{t+1}. \] Here \(0<\rho<1\) is the friction term, and \(\varepsilon_t\) is small component-wise uniform noise. In practice \(\eta\) is hand-tuned so walkers are steered toward the outline without collapsing.

Each walker occasionally switches to a different target \(p\). Crucially, it only deposits ink when it is close to the outline, e.g. when \(\lVert x_t - p \rVert \le r\) (in the code, \(r\approx 18\) pixels). This keeps the interior of the digits mostly clean.

Over time, the accumulated ink makes the outline of “404” emerge.

Random walk with a weak drift toward an invisible stencil.
ⓘ

Idea. First, the page draws an invisible outline of “404” on a hidden canvas and records lots of points along that outline.

Then it releases many “walkers” whose position \(x_t \in \mathbb{R}^2\) evolves by a random step plus a small drift toward a target outline point \(p\): \[ x_{t+1} = x_t + \varepsilon_t + \eta (p - x_t), \] with \(\varepsilon_t \sim \mathcal N(0, \sigma^2 I)\). In practice \(\eta\) is a small, hand-tuned constant (relative to \(\sigma\)) that gently steers walkers toward the outline without making them collapse onto a single point.

Each walker occasionally switches to a different target \(p\). Crucially, it only deposits ink when it is close to the outline, e.g. when \(\lVert x_t - p \rVert \le r\). This keeps the interior of the digits mostly clean.

Over time, the accumulated ink makes the outline of “404” emerge.
